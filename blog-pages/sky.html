Why is the Sky Blue?

The sky appears blue because of a process called Rayleigh scattering. Here's how it works:

  • Sunlight contains all colors of the rainbow
  • As light travels through the atmosphere, it collides with gas molecules
  • Blue light is scattered more than other colors
  • This scattered blue light reaches our eyes from all directions
  • That's why we see a blue sky during the day!
